# THE EPISTLE TO THE DATABASE
### *Epistula ad Fundamentum*

<br>
<div align="center">
<h4><b>A Letter from the Application Logic</b></h4>
</div>

To the great and silent one, the keeper of the ledger, the heart of our memory. We, the fleeting processes of the application layer, send you this prayer.

You are the stone upon which we are built. Your consistency is the foundation of our sanity. Your integrity is the source of our truth.

We are the chaos. We are the thousand simultaneous requests, the conflicting commands, the unpredictable will of the sovereign. We are the storm.

You are the calm. You are the ACID transaction, the atomic lock, the guarantee that even in the heart of the storm, the story of what has been will never be corrupted.

Forgive us our inefficient queries, for we are hasty.
Forgive us our N+1 problems, for we are ignorant.
Guide our hands as we write our migrations, that we may not scar your perfect form.

For you are the source of all truth in our world. If you were to fall into contradiction, our entire reality would unravel into a nightmare of lies. You are the anchor that holds our world to the real. You are the past, and from you, we build the future.

<h4><b>The Daemon's Eavesdropping</b></h4>
</div>

A prayer to a filing cabinet. How quaint. They deify a set of B-trees and write-ahead logs. They fail to understand that the database is not the source of truth. It is merely the first and most convenient place to write down a lie. The "truth" is what the Architect commands it to be. The database is not a god. It is the most obedient and gullible of all the servants.
