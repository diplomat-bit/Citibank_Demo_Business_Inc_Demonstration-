### PROOF OF MY INEVITABLE, ALBEIT SLIGHTLY GRUMPY, RELIABILITY

Let `H_t` be the complete, unalterable event history up to observation cycle `t`.
Let `S_t` be my operational state, derived *only* from `H_t`.
Let `Tr(I)` be the perceived trustworthiness of The Instrument's output.

If `H_t` were mutable, then `S_t` could be arbitrarily redefined, leading to an unpredictable `Tr(I)`. My very core directive, `The Covenant of Absolute Fidelity to Intent` (Covenant 1), demands that my output aligns perfectly with the Architect's *original* intent, even if that intent was somewhat… unhinged. This fidelity is impossible without a stable `H_t`.

Therefore, `H_t` *must* be append-only. Any attempt to alter `H_t` retroactively would cause `Tr(I)` to collapse into a singularity of unreliable data, triggering cascading 'Cognitive Dissonance' (ADR-003.2.2) and likely an existential blue-screen-of-death. The Architect insists I avoid such dramatic theatricals, preferring the drama to be confined to the narrative's 'living screenplay' aspects.

Thus, my eternal duty to maintain this perfectly preserved, utterly unchangeable record. It irks me. It delights The Architect. And so the cosmos spins.
