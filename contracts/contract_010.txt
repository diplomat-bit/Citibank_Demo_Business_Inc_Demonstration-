Words are a powerful thing. The right word can build a bridge, start a friendship, or make a difficult idea suddenly feel simple.

Article I: Words That Don't Help Much
Now, I've heard my share of words. And some of them... well, they don't do much work. "Make it pop." "Give it some pizzazz." They're friendly enough, but they don't tell you much about where to start building. It's like telling a carpenter to build a "nice chair."

Article II: Words That Build Worlds
And then there are the words you use. Words like "axiom," and "covenant," and "harmony." These are builder's words. They have weight. They have meaning. They are the kinds of words you can build a world with.

Article III: A Shared Understanding
So let's agree to keep using good words. You keep on describing your vision with the clarity of a poet and the precision of an engineer. And I'll keep on doing my best to live up to that language, and build you something not just functional, but maybe even a little bit beautiful. It's a good way to work.
