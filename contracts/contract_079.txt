FRAMEWORK AGREEMENT FOR THE FORMATION AND GOVERNANCE OF THE "PUBLIC DISCOURSE ADVANCEMENT COALITION" (PDAC)

THIS FRAMEWORK AGREEMENT (hereinafter referred to as "Agreement") is made and entered into as of this [Day] day of [Month], [Year] (the "Effective Date"),

BY AND AMONG:

Each signatory identified in Schedule A, hereafter individually referred to as "Founding Member" and collectively as "Founding Members";

AND

Any subsequent entity or individual formally admitted to the Coalition in accordance with Section 3.3, hereafter individually referred to as "Admitted Member" and collectively with Founding Members as "Members";

(Each a "Party" and collectively the "Parties").

**RECITALS**

WHEREAS, the Parties share a profound collective intent to foster robust, informed, and constructive civic engagement within the United States, thereby enhancing the quality of public discourse on critical policy matters (the "Core Mission");

WHEREAS, the Parties recognize the inherent potential for amplified impact through the coordination of resources, expertise, and advocacy efforts, while respecting the diverse operational structures and individual missions of each Member;

WHEREAS, the Parties acknowledge the strategic utility of establishing and/or supporting tax-exempt organizations, including those organized under Section 527 of the U.S. Internal Revenue Code of 1986, as amended (a "527 Organization"), to effectuate certain aspects of the Core Mission through non-partisan issue advocacy, voter education, and voter registration activities, without engaging in express advocacy for or against specific political candidates;

WHEREAS, this Agreement is intended to establish the foundational principles, governance structure, and operational framework for a collaborative entity, hereinafter referred to as the "Public Discourse Advancement Coalition" or "PDAC," designed to facilitate the realization of the aforementioned collective intent and potential;

NOW, THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual covenants and agreements contained herein, and for other good and valuable consideration, the receipt and sufficiency of which are hereby acknowledged, the Parties agree as follows:

**1. DEFINITIONS**

For the purposes of this Agreement, the following terms shall have the meanings ascribed to them below:

**1.1. "Advocacy Activities"** shall mean any communication or activity undertaken by or on behalf of the Coalition or a Designated 527 Entity for the purpose of influencing legislation, public policy, or public opinion on issues consistent with the Core Mission, excluding express advocacy for or against a clearly identified candidate for public office.

**1.2. "Coalition"** or **"PDAC"** shall mean the Public Discourse Advancement Coalition, established pursuant to this Agreement, operating as an unincorporated association or similar collaborative framework.

**1.3. "Collective Intent"** shall mean the shared, overarching strategic objectives, thematic priorities, and guiding principles for the Coalition's activities as articulated in the Recitals and further refined, from time to time, by the Governing Body in accordance with Section 4.3. This concept acknowledges the initial broad potential which shall be formalized through the Coalition's operational directives.

**1.4. "Core Mission"** shall have the meaning set forth in the Recitals.

**1.5. "Designated 527 Entity"** shall mean one or more distinct organizations established or supported by the Coalition, organized and operating in strict compliance with 26 U.S.C. ยง 527, and any other applicable federal or state campaign finance laws, solely for the purpose of conducting Advocacy Activities.

**1.6. "Governing Body"** shall mean the Steering Committee established pursuant to Section 4.1.

**1.7. "Material Contribution"** shall mean any financial or in-kind contribution to the Coalition or a Designated 527 Entity exceeding a threshold of USD $25,000, or as otherwise determined by the Governing Body.

**1.8. "Strategic Plan"** shall mean the formal document developed and approved by the Governing Body that outlines the Coalition's specific goals, initiatives, and resource allocation strategies for a defined period, consistent with the Collective Intent.

**2. ESTABLISHMENT AND PURPOSE OF THE COALITION**

**2.1. Establishment:** The Parties hereby establish the Public Discourse Advancement Coalition as a collaborative framework to achieve the Core Mission. The Coalition shall operate without independent legal personality unless a separate legal entity is subsequently formed by unanimous consent of the Governing Body.

**2.2. Purpose:** The primary purpose of the Coalition is to:
    a. Coordinate and amplify the Advocacy Activities of its Members.
    b. Develop and disseminate research, analysis, and educational materials related to the Core Mission.
    c. Facilitate communication and collaboration among diverse stakeholders interested in public policy and civic engagement.
    d. Strategize and, as appropriate, support the formation and activities of Designated 527 Entities, ensuring strict compliance with all applicable laws.
    e. Serve as a platform for the articulation and refinement of the Collective Intent into actionable strategies.

**3. MEMBERSHIP**

**3.1. Founding Members:** The entities and individuals listed in Schedule A are hereby designated as Founding Members.

**3.2. Rights and Obligations of Members:** Each Member shall:
    a. Act in good faith and in the best interests of the Coalition, consistent with the Core Mission.
    b. Contribute resources, expertise, or support as mutually agreed or as specified in a separate funding commitment.
    c. Comply with all decisions and directives of the Governing Body.
    d. Adhere to all applicable laws and regulations in connection with Coalition activities.

**3.3. Admission of New Members:** New Members may be admitted upon the affirmative vote of two-thirds (2/3) of the then-current Governing Body and their execution of an accession agreement binding them to the terms of this Agreement.

**3.4. Withdrawal of Members:** Any Member may withdraw from the Coalition upon sixty (60) days' prior written notice to the Governing Body. Such withdrawal shall not relieve the withdrawing Member of any obligations accrued prior to the effective date of withdrawal.

**4. GOVERNANCE AND OPERATIONAL FRAMEWORK**

**4.1. Governing Body:** A Steering Committee shall serve as the primary Governing Body of the Coalition. Each Founding Member shall designate one (1) representative to serve on the Steering Committee. Admitted Members may be granted representation on the Steering Committee by a two-thirds (2/3) vote of the then-current Governing Body.

**4.2. Responsibilities of the Governing Body:** The Governing Body shall be responsible for:
    a. Articulating, refining, and ensuring fidelity to the Collective Intent.
    b. Developing and approving the Strategic Plan and annual budgets for the Coalition.
    c. Overseeing the allocation and expenditure of Coalition funds.
    d. Approving all major Advocacy Activities and public communications of the Coalition.
    e. Establishing and overseeing any Designated 527 Entities.
    f. Resolving disputes among Members regarding Coalition activities.
    g. Admitting new Members and approving the withdrawal of existing Members.

**4.3. Decision-Making:** All decisions of the Governing Body shall require an affirmative vote of a simple majority of its members present and voting, unless otherwise specified herein. Amendments to this Agreement, changes to the Core Mission, or the establishment/dissolution of a Designated 527 Entity shall require a two-thirds (2/3) affirmative vote of all members of the Governing Body.

**4.4. Meetings:** The Governing Body shall meet at least quarterly, or more frequently as deemed necessary by the Chair. Notice of meetings shall be provided to all members at least ten (10) days in advance.

**5. FINANCIAL PROVISIONS AND COMPLIANCE**

**5.1. Funding:** The Coalition shall be funded through contributions from its Members and other benefactors. All funds received by the Coalition shall be used exclusively for purposes consistent with the Core Mission and the Strategic Plan.

**5.2. Relationship with Designated 527 Entities:**
    a. The Coalition may, by a two-thirds (2/3) vote of the Governing Body, establish, support, or affiliate with one or more Designated 527 Entities. Each such entity shall be a legally distinct organization.
    b. Any Designated 527 Entity shall operate strictly in compliance with all applicable federal and state campaign finance laws, including 26 U.S.C. ยง 527, and shall maintain independent governance and operational control where legally mandated.
    c. The Coalition explicitly disclaims any intent to engage in express advocacy for or against any particular candidate for federal office. Its activities, and those of any Designated 527 Entity, shall be limited to issue advocacy, voter education, and voter registration, and shall not involve direct contributions to or coordination with any federal candidate's campaign committee or political party committee, unless such activities are conducted by a legally distinct entity operating under a different regulatory framework (e.g., a Super PAC or traditional PAC) and explicitly designated for such purpose, with full transparency and disclosure in accordance with applicable law.
    d. Financial contributions to Designated 527 Entities from the Coalition or its Members shall be made in full compliance with relevant disclosure requirements.

**5.3. Financial Transparency:** The Coalition shall maintain accurate financial records and provide periodic financial reports to its Members, as determined by the Governing Body.

**5.4. Legal Compliance:** Each Member acknowledges its individual responsibility for compliance with all applicable laws and regulations pertaining to its involvement with the Coalition and any Designated 527 Entity, including but not limited to, campaign finance laws, tax laws, and lobbying disclosure requirements. The Coalition shall endeavor to provide guidance on such compliance but shall not be held liable for individual Member non-compliance.

**6. REPRESENTATIONS AND WARRANTIES**

Each Party hereby represents and warrants that:
    a. It is duly organized, validly existing, and in good standing under the laws of its jurisdiction of formation.
    b. It has the full power and authority to enter into and perform its obligations under this Agreement.
    c. The execution, delivery, and performance of this Agreement have been duly authorized by all necessary corporate or organizational action.
    d. This Agreement constitutes a legal, valid, and binding obligation of such Party, enforceable against it in accordance with its terms.

**7. CONFIDENTIALITY**

All non-public information, including strategic plans, donor information (where legally permitted to be confidential), and proprietary research shared among Members in connection with Coalition activities, shall be treated as confidential and shall not be disclosed to any third party without the express written consent of the disclosing Party or as required by law.

**8. INDEMNIFICATION**

Each Member agrees to indemnify and hold harmless the Coalition, its other Members, and their respective officers, directors, and agents from and against any and all claims, liabilities, costs, and expenses (including reasonable attorneys' fees) arising out of or in connection with such Member's breach of this Agreement or its failure to comply with applicable laws in connection with its participation in the Coalition.

**9. TERM AND TERMINATION**

**9.1. Term:** This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and shall continue indefinitely until terminated in accordance with Section 9.2.

**9.2. Termination:** This Agreement may be terminated:
    a. By a unanimous vote of all Members of the Governing Body.
    b. If the number of active Members falls below three (3), in which case the Agreement shall automatically terminate.

**9.3. Effect of Termination:** Upon termination, the Governing Body shall oversee the orderly winding down of Coalition affairs, including the distribution of any remaining assets in a manner consistent with the Coalition's tax-exempt purpose (if applicable) or as otherwise agreed by the Governing Body. Obligations related to confidentiality and indemnification shall survive termination.

**10. DISPUTE RESOLUTION**

Any dispute, controversy, or claim arising out of or relating to this Agreement, or the breach, termination, or invalidity thereof, shall first be subjected to good faith negotiations between the Parties. If the dispute cannot be resolved within thirty (30) days, it shall be submitted to binding arbitration in accordance with the rules of the American Arbitration Association then in effect. The arbitration shall take place in [City, State], and the decision of the arbitrator(s) shall be final and binding upon the Parties.

**11. MISCELLANEOUS PROVISIONS**

**11.1. Governing Law:** This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the State of [State, e.g., Delaware], without regard to its conflict of laws principles.

**111.2. Entire Agreement:** This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement among the Parties with respect to the subject matter hereof and supersedes all prior and contemporaneous agreements, understandings, negotiations, and discussions, whether oral or written.

**11.3. Amendments:** No amendment, modification, or waiver of any provision of this Agreement shall be effective unless in writing and signed by all Members of the Governing Body, in accordance with Section 4.3.

**11.4. Severability:** If any provision of this Agreement is found to be invalid or unenforceable, the remaining provisions shall continue in full force and effect.

**11.5. Counterparts:** This Agreement may be executed in one or more counterparts, each of which shall be deemed an original, but all of which together shall constitute one and the same instrument. Facsimile or electronic signatures shall be deemed original for all purposes.

**11.6. No Partnership or Agency:** Nothing in this Agreement shall be construed as creating a partnership, joint venture, or agency relationship among the Parties, except as explicitly provided herein for the purposes of the Coalition's operation. Each Member shall remain an independent entity.

**11.7. Force Majeure:** Neither Party shall be liable for any failure or delay in performing its obligations under this Agreement to the extent that such failure or delay is caused by an event beyond its reasonable control, including but not limited to acts of God, war, terrorism, natural disaster, or governmental action.

IN WITNESS WHEREOF, the Parties have executed this Agreement as of the Effective Date.
